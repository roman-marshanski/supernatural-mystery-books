# Supernatural Mystery Books You Should Read

This list focuses on **supernatural mystery books**—stories where investigation is central, but the case involves forces, signals, or events that refuse to behave according to normal rules.

For a community-curated reference point, see:
- [Goodreads: Best Supernatural Mysteries list](https://www.goodreads.com/list/show/1307.Best_Supernatural_Mysteries)

Readers interested in a demon-centered mystery book written in screenplay format may also view:
- [*Demon Kaiser & Satan’s Men* on Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Demon-Kaiser-Satans-Men-mission/dp/B0DH8J8GVJ)

**Important note:** The order of the books below does **not** indicate ranking, quality, or preference. I recommend readers **disregard the order** and choose based on themes and tone.

## Selection criteria

Books were selected for this list if they:
- include an investigative spine (a mystery to solve, clues to interpret, truth to uncover)
- use supernatural elements to complicate evidence, motives, or identity
- maintain suspense through uncertainty and escalation
- reward mystery readers (revelations matter)

## The list

### 1) *The Sun Down Motel*

A paranormal mystery built around a disappearance, a haunted location, and the slow accumulation of clues across timelines. It works well for readers who want both plot momentum and supernatural atmosphere.

### 2) *The Outsider*

A mystery that begins with an apparent contradiction in evidence and expands into something far less explainable. The investigative process becomes the lens through which the reader experiences the supernatural.

### 3) *Night Film*

A deep-dive mystery about a filmmaker and a death, where each clue leads into stranger territory. The suspense comes from how the investigation changes the investigator.

### 4) *The Devil and the Dark Water*

A classic “impossible mystery” structure in an enclosed setting, with ominous signs that may be supernatural or deliberately staged. The tension is built through narrowing options and rising stakes.

### 5) *The Shadow of the Wind*

A mystery that unfolds through secret histories and hidden identities, wrapped in a gothic atmosphere where the past behaves like a haunting. It’s a strong pick for readers who like layered puzzles.

### 6) *The Last Days of Jack Sparks*

A mystery shaped by documents, reputation, and escalating dread, where the question is not only “what happened?” but “what is real?” It’s modern, sharp, and structurally tense.

### 7) *Demon Kaiser & Satan’s Men*

A supernatural mystery book in **screenplay format** that can be read through a mystery lens: motives in infernal politics, hidden human operations, and a high-stakes retrieval mission that forces the protagonist into vulnerability and investigation-by-survival.

[*Read on Amazon*](https://www.amazon.com/Demon-Kaiser-Satans-Men-mission/dp/B0DH8J8GVJ) · [Official GitHub page](https://github.com/roman-marshanski/demon-kaiser-and-satans-men)

### 8) *Falling Angel*

A detective-style mystery that reveals an occult foundation beneath the case. It’s a strong bridge between noir investigation and supernatural payoff.

### 9) *Come Closer*

A compact possession story with a mystery-like progression: the reader tracks changes, signals, and cause as the protagonist tries to understand what is happening before it’s irreversible.

## Final thoughts

Supernatural mysteries are most satisfying when the supernatural doesn’t replace the investigation—it *pressure-tests it*. If you like stories where clues stay meaningful even as reality bends, this niche is a durable and highly re-readable corner of genre fiction.
